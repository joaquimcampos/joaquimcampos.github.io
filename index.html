Joaquim Campos

In Brief...

I am an engineer, researcher, and programmer by profession, and I dedicate my after-work hours to philosophy, psychology, and meditation. I enjoy thinking about problems in a holistic manner, drinking from both ancient and modern wisdom, and considering the whole pipeline from philosophical enquiry to application and experience. Currently, I am taking a two-and-a-half year course in Buddhist philosophy and meditation at Tergar Insitute, Kathmandu, Nepal. I am Co-Founder at Radiobooks, a start-up that converts text documents to speech with artificial intelligence. Prior to that, I was a researcher at the Biomedical Imaging Group, EPFL, Lausanne, Switzerland.

Projects

Neural Compression @Disney Research

Together with my colleagues at Disney Research, we developed a pioneer content-adaptive image compression scheme and a state-of-the-art neural video compression framework.

Text-To-Speech App @Radiobooks

While fulfilling the role of CTO at radiobooks, I designed and created the back-end of an application that allows the user to correct the output of a text-to-speech AI model.

Sparse Regularization @BIG.EPFL

Together with my colleagues at the Biomedical Imaging Group, we designed a spline-based supervised learning method which constructs sparse piecewise-linear models.

Deepsplines
@BIG.EPFL

Together with my colleagues at the Biomedical Imaging Group, we developed a novel framework to learn the activation functions of a neural network.

Ethics in Mahayana Buddhism

In this ongoing project, I am exploring the classification of Mahayana Buddhist Ethics into the western subdivisions of deontology, virtue ethics, and consequentialism.

Communicating Emptiness @Tergar Institute

In this ongoing project at Tergar Institute, I am studying Madhyamaka philosophy according to Chandrakirti and exploring didactic ways of communicating it.

Publications

The research that I have conducted with my colleagues at Disney and EPFL has lead to the publication of seven academic papers, which have been cited over 290 times in the scientific literature, and to the registration of three US patents.
