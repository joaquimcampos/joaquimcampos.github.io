In Brief...

I am an engineer, researcher, and programmer by profession, and I dedicate my after-work hours to philosophy, psychology, and meditation. I enjoy thinking about problems in a holistic manner, drinking from both ancient and modern wisdom, and considering the whole pipeline from philosophical enquiry to application and experience. Currently, I am taking a two-and-a-half year course in Buddhist philosophy and meditation at Tergar Insitute, Kathmandu, Nepal.

Studies

BSc. in Electrical and Computer Engineering, University of Lisbon

This course was taught by the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, University of Lisbon, Portugal. Grade: 16.4/20.

This course was taught by the School of Computer Science and Communication Sciences, EPFL, Switzerland. Work

Image and Video Compression, Disney Research

While working at Disney Research, Zurich, under the supervision of Christopher Schroers, my colleagues and I developed the first content-adaptive neural image compression scheme and constructed a state-of-the-art neural video compression framework. Our projects can be found here.

Neural Networks and Regularization, Biomedical Imaging Group

While working as a research assistant at the Biomedical Imaging Group, EPFL, Switzerland, under the supervision of Professor Michael Unser, my colleagues and I conducted research on Supervised Learning with Sparsity-Promoting Regularization; our projects can be found here. During that time, I also worked as a teaching assistant for the courses Signals and Systems I and II.

Text-to-Speech, Radiobooks

As Co-Founder and CTO of Radiobooks, I helped develop an app to convert text documents to speech with AI which gives users ample control over the generated audio, allowing them to customize it to fit their requirements. The description of this project can be found here.
